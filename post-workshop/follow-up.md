# Post-workshop

What keeps the workshop alive after everyone flies home. The goal is the same as the
day itself: people *using* this, not just remembering a demo.

## The follow-up check-in

A few days out, invite attendees to run a **third** check-in — "did you get closer to
your goals?" It reuses the [`coach-checkin`](../skills/coach-checkin/) participant id,
so the board (or a new dashboard) can show real before → after → *a week later* movement:
did the hooks and scheduled tasks they built actually stick?

## Roadmap

- [ ] **Follow-up phase.** Add a `follow` phase to the coach contract + board (mirrors `pre`/`post`).
- [ ] **Workshop RAG endpoint.** A small service that answers "how do I do X from the
      workshop" afterward — same spirit as the GHX glossary/RAG that's still getting traffic.
      Captures where people get stuck *by them asking*, not by us scanning.
- [ ] **Keep the repo.** Attendees keep the repo, the skills, and their scheduled task.
      Make sure the README's quickstart works cold, weeks later, on a fresh machine.

## Privacy

Every post-workshop touchpoint follows the same hard rule as the live coach:
**volunteered answers only, nothing scanned.** See [`../docs/design.md`](../docs/design.md).
