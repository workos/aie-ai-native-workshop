# Block 4 — Scheduled tasks (it runs without you)

**~8 min · the payoff.** Everything so far ran because *they* were sitting there. This block cuts the cord: the work happens on a schedule, while they sleep.

## The arc

1. **Schedule the thing you just built.** In Block 1–3 they produced a real output — a bug fixed, gated, and written up. Now schedule it:
   > "Schedule this to run every Monday morning: pull the latest, fix the top lint issues, run the gates, and draft a short post about what changed."

   The cron-style scheduled task becomes the artifact they take home. The loop they ran by hand now runs itself.

2. **Make it tangible.** The win has to be felt, not described:
   - Schedule a near-term run so they see it fire before they leave, **or**
   - Have the scheduled task ping them (a message / notification) when it completes, so the "it ran without me" moment lands on their phone.

3. **The closing check-in.** Each person runs:
   > "Run my closing check-in."

   The [`coach-checkin`](../skills/coach-checkin/) skill asks what they wired up and what they'll automate next, and posts it to the [board](../board/). The room watches its dots **migrate from toil to leverage** — and the marquee number lands: *the total engineering-hours/week the room just reclaimed.*

## What they leave with

- A scheduled task running in their own environment — the repo and the habit, not just a demo.
- The full operator stack assembled once, end to end: **voice → loops & goals → verification gates → scheduled tasks.**
- A number, on the board, for what that stack is worth to them per week.

## Facilitator notes

- This is the thematic tie-together — make sure the scheduled task is the *same* work they did live, now automated. The story has to close the loop it opened in Block 1.
- The closing board state is the recording's money shot: before→after, with the hours-reclaimed number front and center.
